# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Project spaces should be safe, respectful, and useful. Participate with empathy, assume good faith while evaluating evidence, welcome different experience levels, and criticize ideas or code rather than people.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, publishing private data, or sustained disruption. Never post credentials, private repository content, personal paths, or vulnerability details in public project spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close interactions, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior threatens people or the project. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and avoid exposing affected people.

After publication, report conduct concerns through the private maintainer contact named by the repository. Until that contact is configured, do not disclose sensitive details publicly. Good-faith reports will be handled as confidentially as practical; retaliation is not acceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository discussions, review, documentation, community channels, and public representation of the project.
